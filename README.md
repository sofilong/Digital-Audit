<h1>Digital Audit for Operational Improvement</h1>
<p>
  The Company dilemma when it comes to share critical information with a Business Partner: What to share, where are the files from "X"project?
  what is the IP policies when it comes to share information. Or the IT guy raising the flag to the Supervisor on the increase of data needs vs current storage availability.
  Either getting another deal with a Cloud Service to ensure storage capacity, or facing a challenge on how to cope with the data fast-grow pace.
  For both situations, implementing an Digital Audit would help map the Company's digital assets (files) to assess current storage, what and where critical assets are
  to safely and securely make available for business continuity, vs what are no longer required that can be safely remove.
</p>

<h2>How is this project useful?</h2>
<p>
  Digital Audit falls under data governance and process improvement. Becuase it evaluates how data is collected, stored, secured, accessed, and used
  in workflows, system behaviour that could cause bottlenecks hence impact system performance.By diagnosting on how effectively a Company is managing
  its digital assess and the storage needs it can indentify what asset to keep, locked and backup vs asset that are no longer needed and can be safely and securely deprecated.
  Also, it helps on economical analysis on storage usage (what can be refactured by removing unecessary data), avoiding extra spend on extra storage services while maximizing system
  operational efficiency and capabilities. <img src="Digital Audit.png" alt="Dashboard viz">
</p>
